Visualizing the transformation of a uniform distribution

Consider sunlight (photons) falling uniformly on a roof. The part turned towards the sun will be hotter---where the propability of a photon hitting an area is higher. The roof transforms the uniform horizontal distribution of photons to a non-uniform vertical distribution of temperature---as estimated by the histogram. If we turn the screen 90 degrees, the amplitude of the histogram depends on the slope of the rotated roof. Or, for a given histogram the rotated roof is the integral of its amplitude. This is the basic way in which a uniform probability distribution is transformed to an arbitrary distribution. Try changing the roof by dragging the black dot.
